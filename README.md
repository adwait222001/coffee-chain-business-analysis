# Coffee Chain Sales & Financial Performance SQL Analysis

## Overview

This project analyzes a multi-state coffee chain's sales, profitability, operational expenses, and future business performance using SQL and Google BigQuery.

The dataset contains store-level business data including Sales, Profit, Cost of Goods Sold (COGS), Marketing expenditure, Other Expenses, Product information, Market classifications, and Geographic regions.

The objective of this project is to perform exploratory data analysis, evaluate financial performance, identify growth opportunities, and generate actionable business recommendations.

The analysis focuses on:

* Sales performance
* Profitability analysis
* Product performance
* Market-wise analysis
* State-wise business performance
* Operational efficiency
* Future sales and profit forecasting

---

## Business Problem

The coffee chain operates across multiple states and market regions with varying levels of sales performance and profitability.

The goal of this analysis is to understand:

* Which products generate the highest revenue and profit
* How sales and profitability vary across states and markets
* The impact of COGS and expenses on profitability
* Performance differences between major and small markets
* Regional business performance
* Future sales and profit trends
* Opportunities for improving operational efficiency and cash flow

The project also aims to identify high-performing stores, profitable products, and growth opportunities that can support long-term business expansion.

## Key Business Insights

* Espresso (Beans) recorded the highest total COGS expenditure (23,744), indicating that it requires the largest inventory investment among all product categories.
* Tea (Leaves) recorded the lowest total COGS expenditure (19,170), representing the smallest inventory investment.
* Espresso contributes the highest share of revenue among all product categories, making it the primary driver of sales performance.
* The dataset classifies states into Major Markets and Small Markets, enabling comparison of market size and business performance.
* California is the highest-performing state among Major Markets with total sales of 23,032.
* Nevada is the highest-performing state among Small Markets with total sales of 14,822.
* The market structure is divided into four regions: West, South, East, and Central, allowing regional performance analysis.
* Columbian Coffee demonstrates strong ingredient-level economics with high sales, strong gross margins, and significant profitability contribution.
* The West region generates the highest total sales and profit among all sectors while also recording the highest total COGS.
* Several stores consistently achieve strong operating profits, indicating effective cost management and operational efficiency.
* Future sales and profit projections indicate that Major Markets are expected to continue driving business growth and financial performance.

---

## Recommendations

* Prioritize Espresso (Beans) as a strategic product by optimizing procurement processes and negotiating better supplier pricing to improve margins.
* Increase marketing investments in Major Markets to maximize growth opportunities and strengthen market penetration.
* Improve operational efficiency through better inventory management, supplier negotiations, and cost-control initiatives aimed at reducing COGS.
* Reinvest excess cash generated by highly profitable stores into underperforming stores and emerging growth markets.
* Expand business operations and targeted marketing efforts within the West region to capitalize on its strong sales and profitability.
* Focus on second-highest-performing stores within each state through strategic promotions and marketing campaigns to accelerate growth.
* Optimize procurement and inventory management for high-performing ingredients such as Columbian Coffee and Espresso to improve cash flow and profitability.
* Utilize future sales and profit forecasts to guide strategic resource allocation, expansion planning, and long-term business growth initiatives.
